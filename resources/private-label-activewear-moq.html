Buyer guide

Private Label Activewear MOQ Guide

MOQ depends on fabric availability, color, logo method, trims, labels, packaging, size range, and whether you choose light custom, ODM, or full OEM production.

Ask About MOQActivewear Manufacturer

Direct answer

What affects MOQ for private label activewear?

The largest MOQ drivers are custom fabric dyeing, custom knitting, special trims, complex logo methods, packaging quantity, color count, size breakdown, and whether each style uses the same fabric.

Lower MOQ route

Use existing fabric, proven silhouettes, limited colors, heat transfer logo, standard trims, and simple private label packaging.

Higher MOQ route

Custom-dyed fabric, seamless knitting, special hardware, custom waistband, unique print, multiple colors, and retail packaging often raise MOQ.

Sample route

Samples can confirm fabric, fit, logo, labels, and packaging before deciding if bulk quantities should be split by style, color, or size.

Buyer tip

Share total quantity, color count, size range, target price, and launch plan early so the supplier can suggest a practical route.

FAQ

MOQ questions

Can a new brand start with small quantities?

Small test orders may be possible when the style, fabric, color, and branding route are kept simple. Full custom fabric or packaging usually needs higher quantities.

Does custom logo increase MOQ?

Logo method affects MOQ less than custom fabric or packaging, but complex embroidery, silicone badges, and multiple placements can affect cost and sample time.

Can colors be mixed within one order?

Colors can often be mixed, but each color adds planning complexity. Custom dyeing usually has its own minimum quantity.

What should I send to check MOQ?

Send product list, reference images, target quantity, color count, size range, fabric preference, logo files, packaging needs, and delivery country.
