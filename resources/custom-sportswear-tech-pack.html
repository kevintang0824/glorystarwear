Custom Sportswear Tech Pack Checklist

A clear tech pack helps suppliers quote faster, sample more accurately, control quality, and reduce mistakes in fabric, fit, logo placement, labels, packaging, and shipment.

Direct answer

What should a sportswear tech pack include?

A practical sportswear tech pack should include product references, measurement chart, fabric and trim requirements, logo artwork, color references, stitching notes, labels, packaging, size range, tolerance, and inspection requirements.

Product details

Style name, category, front and back references, construction notes, fit target, size range, and wearer market.

Material details

Fabric composition, weight, stretch, handfeel, opacity, compression, color reference, trims, pads, zippers, drawcords, and mesh zones.

Branding details

Logo file, placement, size, method, color, neck label, care label, woven label, hangtag, barcode, and sponsor artwork.

Production details

Quantity, size ratio, packaging, carton rules, tolerance, inspection standard, sample notes, delivery country, and timeline.

FAQ

Tech pack questions

Can I get a quote without a complete tech pack?

Yes. You can start with reference images, target product list, quantity, logo files, fabric preference, size range, packaging needs, and delivery country.

Do I need vector logo files?

Vector files such as AI, PDF, EPS, or SVG are best for accurate logo placement, heat transfer, embroidery, labels, and packaging artwork.

Should packaging be included in the tech pack?

Yes. Packaging affects cost, preparation time, carton planning, barcode labels, size stickers, and retail presentation.

Can tech pack details be revised after sampling?

Yes. Sample comments should update measurements, fabric, logo placement, labels, packaging, and tolerance before bulk approval.
