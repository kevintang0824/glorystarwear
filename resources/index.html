Sourcing workflow

Move from product idea to comparable supplier quote

Use the resources in sequence when the project is early, or jump directly to the stage where information is missing.

  1. Define the commercial brief

    State the customer, market, product scope, target position, quantity plan, brand requirements, and launch priority.

  2. Choose the development route

    Decide which styles have controlled specifications and which need supplier support, base-style adaptation, or material options.

  3. Build the product and material record

    Prepare tech packs or structured briefs with coded fabrics, measurements, grade rules, construction, artwork, labels, packaging, acceptance rules, and open questions.

  4. Test MOQ feasibility

    Ask what sets the minimum for each style, color, material, decoration, label, and packaging component.

  5. Verify the supplier

    Review relevant capabilities, certificate evidence, sample and QC process, comparable project material, and communication ownership.

  6. Control variable order data

    For sized or personalized programs, release one approved size chart, roster, artwork package, packing route, and revision history.

  7. Compare the same scope

    Send one controlled brief to shortlisted suppliers and record assumptions, exclusions, sample stages, approvals, and packing requirements.
