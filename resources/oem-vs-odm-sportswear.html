Buyer guide

OEM vs ODM Sportswear Manufacturing

OEM starts from your controlled specification. ODM starts from a commercial brief and requires more supplier input. The right route depends on what is already decided and what still needs development.

Discuss Your RouteCustomization Options

Direct answer

What is the difference between OEM and ODM sportswear?

In an OEM project, the buyer owns the product direction and supplies a usable specification such as a tech pack, measurements, artwork, and packaging rules. In an ODM project, the buyer supplies a market goal and references, while the supplier helps turn those inputs into a product plan. Because factories use these terms differently, confirm the exact deliverables before comparing quotes.

Updated July 14, 2026For private label activewear and teamwear buyersMore buyer resources

Choose OEM when

You have controlled specifications, measurement charts, approved artwork, label files, packaging instructions, and a clear approval process.

Choose ODM when

You have a target customer, product references, price position, quantity plan, and launch goal but need help defining the product.

Choose light custom when

You want to adapt a proven base style through color, logo, labels, simple trim changes, and branded packaging.

Use a hybrid route when

Core styles follow your tech packs while supporting styles, trims, fabric options, or packaging use supplier development support.

Working method

How to choose the route in six steps

A buyer can make this decision before discussing price by auditing the information already available for each style.

  1. List the styles separately

    Create one row per product. A collection may use OEM for leggings, light custom for tees, and ODM for a jacket.

  2. Mark what is approved

    Record whether fit, measurements, fabric, construction, colors, artwork, labels, and packaging are fixed or still open.

  3. Define the wearer and market

    State the activity, target customer, selling region, intended retail position, and any required sizing or labeling rules.

  4. Separate must-haves from options

    Protect the details that define the product while allowing alternatives for materials, trims, or processes that can change.

  5. Ask for named deliverables

    Confirm who creates patterns, measurement charts, artwork layouts, labels, packaging files, sample comments, and bulk standards.

  6. Approve one source of truth

    After sampling, consolidate the final specification and signed comments so production does not rely on scattered messages.

Useful in practice

A hybrid route often fits a mixed collection

A route does not need to cover the entire order. It can be assigned style by style, as long as the scope is written into the development sheet.

  • An activewear brand can provide an OEM legging tech pack, adapt a proven sports bra, and request ODM support for a warm-up layer.
  • A teamwear buyer can control jersey artwork and roster data while using supplier options for shorts, socks, warm-ups, labels, and player packing.
  • A launch can begin with standard fabric and trims, then move selected styles to custom materials after fit and demand are validated.

Quote preparation

Send enough information to compare suppliers fairly

Quotes are difficult to compare when one supplier assumes standard materials and another assumes custom development. Send the same brief to every shortlisted supplier.

FAQ

OEM and ODM questions

Is ODM always more expensive than OEM?

No. Cost depends on the product and the development work included. An ODM project may include more early-stage support, while an incomplete OEM specification can also require extra sampling and correction.

Can a buyer start with reference images only?

Yes, as an initial ODM discussion. Add the target customer, product list, quantity, market, price position, logo files, fabric preferences, packaging needs, and launch goal so the references are not interpreted in isolation.

Can OEM and ODM be combined?

Yes. Assign a route to each style or component. Keep the scope explicit so everyone knows which specifications are controlled by the buyer and which decisions the supplier is expected to develop.

Which route is best for a new sportswear brand?

Many new brands begin with light custom or a focused ODM brief because they do not yet have complete specifications. The best route is the one that matches the team's ability to define, review, and approve product details.
