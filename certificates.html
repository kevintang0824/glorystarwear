Sportswear Certificates and Compliance

This page explains what private label sportswear buyers should verify for fabric certificates, factory audits, label compliance, packaging documents, and product testing before bulk production.

Ask for DocumentsFactory Profile

Important note

Certificate claims should be verified per project

Different buyers need different standards by country, marketplace, brand policy, fabric origin, and product category. GloryStarWear should confirm available documents, certified material options, and testing requirements before sample approval.

Factory audit documents

Buyers may request factory profile, business details, production process photos, quality workflow, audit documents, and video call verification.

Fabric certificates

For recycled, organic, OEKO-TEX, GRS, or special performance claims, confirm supplier documents and fabric availability before quoting.

Label compliance

Care label, fiber content, size label, country of origin, hangtag claims, barcode labels, and packaging text should match destination market requirements.

Testing route

Colorfastness, shrinkage, pilling, opacity, stretch recovery, print durability, and packaging tests can be planned when required by the buyer.

Document checklist

What to prepare before bulk production

Use this checklist as a practical buyer verification layer. Add real certificates or test reports when available.

01

Buyer standard

Target country, retail channel, marketplace policy, brand compliance manual, and required test scope.

02

Material proof

Fabric composition, material supplier, certified fabric request, lab dip, color card, trim documents, and care label direction.

03

Product proof

Approved sample, size chart, measurement tolerance, logo method, packaging sample, and final artwork files.

04

Shipment proof

Packing list, carton marks, barcode labels, SKU sorting, final inspection notes, and delivery documents.

Buyer FAQ

Compliance questions

Can I request certified fabrics for private label activewear?

Yes. Tell us the exact standard and market requirement first, then we can confirm available certified fabric options and documents before sampling.

Can factory or audit documents be shared?

Project-related factory profile materials, workflow information, and verification documents can be discussed during the quote process. Sensitive documents may require buyer details or an NDA.

What label information should buyers confirm?

Confirm fiber content, care instructions, size system, country of origin wording, barcode rules, packaging claims, and any marketplace-specific label requirements.

Can product testing be arranged?

Testing can be planned around fabric, print durability, shrinkage, colorfastness, pilling, stretch recovery, opacity, and packaging requirements when the buyer specifies the standard.

Need compliance documents for a quote?

Send your target market, product type, fabric requirement, standard name, quantity, and delivery country so the right documentation route can be confirmed.
